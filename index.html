I am a PhD student in the Flowers AI & CogSci Team at Inria Bordeaux, supervised by Pierre-Yves Oudeyer and Clément Moulin-Frier. I develop curiosity-driven AI methods for assisted scientific discovery, for Artificial Life and biologically aligned substrates in particular. My broader interests sit at the intersection of machine learning, evolutionary algorithms, reinforcement learning, and cognitive science, and I am fascinated by emergence in all its forms.

Research

Complex systems — biological and computational — produce striking behaviors that we still cannot reliably discover, characterize, or steer. A major focus of my thesis is to create artificial scientists: AI tools that autonomously navigate vast spaces of possible simulations to discover phenomena that are meaningful to human researchers.

I work on agents that learn what to look for. I draw from diverse domains in AI, autotelic reinforcement learning, generative/foundation models, and evolutionary search, and adapt them to autonomously propose goals, discover novel phenomena, and steer dynamical systems toward target behaviors — with hand-crafted or unsupervised feature descriptors. Recent work (under review) demonstrates closed-loop agents that generalize zero-shot to out-of-distribution control targets in continuous cellular automata, in collaboration with the Levin Lab at Tufts.

I see this line of work as an early step toward foundation models as engines of open-ended scientific discovery: agents that explore systems they have never seen before, surface non-obvious phenomena, and propose hypotheses under the guidance and alignment of human scientists. I am fascinated by cellular automata and use them as a tractable, but unbounded testbed.

Beyond building these tools, I am drawn to the questions they uncover: what are the minimal conditions for open-ended evolution, the process by which simple interactions generate ever-more complex forms? What do concepts like memory, agency, and intelligence mean in systems where complexity arises from the bottom up?

I strive to bring my methods to tasks other researchers find meaningful. To this end, I have a fruitful, ongoing collaboration with the Levin Lab at Tufts.
